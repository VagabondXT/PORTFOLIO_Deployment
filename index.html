The Bigger Picture

Life Science: Simulating
the world to teach it

In 2021, while conducting field research for my BSA conference paper, I sat in classrooms across rural Bangladesh. Students were bright, curious, and hungry — but the curriculum gave them textbooks about careers they'd never observed and systems they'd never touched.

That experience planted a seed: what if a student in Sylhet could explore being a marine biologist, a civil engineer, or an economist — not through reading, but by actually doing it in simulation?

Life Science is my long-term vision: a virtual curriculum platform where students navigate simulated ecosystems, economies, and societies. Decisions have consequences. Failure is free. Learning is undeniable. Built on the same AI and simulation technology I've been developing professionally.

  • 🌍 Designed for under-resourced schools — mobile-first, low-bandwidth, multilingual
  • 🔬 Evidence-based: curriculum maps to national standards, outcomes tracked with statistical rigor
  • 🤖 AI tutors powered by LLMs adapt to each student's learning style and pace
